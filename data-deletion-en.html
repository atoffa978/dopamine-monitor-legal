Data deletion

English translation provided in good faith for accessibility. The Italian version remains the legally binding text in case of any discrepancy or legal dispute.

Dopamine Monitor is a passive self-observation app: the vast majority of data concerning you (usage statistics, notification counts, list of installed apps) never leaves your device. Only if you have explicitly opted in to the optional telemetry, the app sends an anonymous daily summary (0-100 scores, no app names) to our European servers.

1. Delete local data on the device

Open the app → Settings → Erase all data. The operation immediately and irreversibly deletes from the device:

  • all accumulated daily statistics (up to 60 days of history)
  • user preferences (language, hours, category overrides)
  • the random install_id generated the first time you opened the app
  • the count of "friction" popups triggered and respected

Alternatively, uninstalling the app produces the same effect: the Android system automatically removes all associated data.

2. Delete telemetry data on our servers

If you have opted in to the optional telemetry, our servers (Scaleway, Paris) may hold the anonymous daily summaries associated with your install_id. To request earlier deletion (by default these are automatically removed after 24 months):

  1. Retrieve your install_id: open the app → Settings → Telemetry → Show installation ID. It is a UUID in the format xxxxxxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx.
  2. Write an email to dopamine.monitor.dev@gmail.com with subject "GDPR data deletion request" and your install_id in the body.
  3. You will receive confirmation within 30 days from receipt of the request, as required by Art. 17 GDPR.

No other personal data is required (no name, no document). The install_id is sufficient because it is the only identifier we link to telemetry data.

3. What remains after deletion

Aggregated statistical distributions (e.g. "the average Dopa Index of users after 30 days of use is X") may remain on our systems even after deletion of your individual payloads, because they are aggregated anonymous data containing no records referable to you.

4. Legal references

Right to erasure: Art. 17 of EU Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR). For all other rights (access, rectification, restriction, objection, portability, complaint to the Italian Data Protection Authority), see Privacy Policy §7.

5. Contact

For any question: dopamine.monitor.dev@gmail.com
