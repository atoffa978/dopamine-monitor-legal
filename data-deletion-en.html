Data deletion

English translation provided in good faith for accessibility. The Italian version remains the legally binding text in case of any discrepancy or legal dispute.

Dopamine Monitor is a passive self-observation app: the vast majority of data concerning you (usage statistics, notification counts, list of installed apps) never leaves your device. Only if you have explicitly opted in to the optional telemetry, the app sends a pseudonymized daily summary (0-100 scores, no app names) to our European servers.

1. Delete local data on the device

Open the app → Settings → Erase all data. The operation immediately and irreversibly deletes from the device:

  • all accumulated daily statistics (up to 60 days of history)
  • user preferences (language, hours, category overrides)
  • the random install_id generated the first time you opened the app
  • the count of "friction" popups triggered and respected

Alternatively, uninstalling the app produces the same effect: the Android system automatically removes all associated data.

2. Delete telemetry data on our servers

If you have opted in to the optional telemetry, our servers (Scaleway, Paris) may hold pseudonymized daily summaries referable to your device. To request earlier deletion (by default these are automatically removed after 24 months):

  1. Retrieve your install_id: open the app → Settings → Telemetry section → Show installation ID button → Copy button. It is a 32-character hexadecimal UUID, for example 7b3f2c914d8e4123abcd1234567890ef.
  2. Write an email to dopamine.monitor.dev@gmail.com with subject "GDPR data deletion request" and your install_id in the body.
  3. Server-side we recompute the set of possible dedup_hash values for that device (one for each observation day, up to 730) and delete all corresponding records from the database.
  4. You will receive confirmation within 30 days from receipt of the request, as required by Art. 17 GDPR.

No other personal data is required (no name, no document). The install_id is sufficient because it is the only input that allows us to reconstruct your dedup_hashes and identify the corresponding records.

3. Why we need the install_id

The data we receive is pseudonymized, not anonymous.

Our server does not know who you are: we receive neither your email, nor your name, nor your install_id in clear. We receive only a dedup_hash for each transmitted day — a 16-character hexadecimal string generated by the app as SHA-256(install_id + "|" + observation_day), truncated. By itself this string is apparently random and we cannot link it either to your install_id or across different days.

When you send us your install_id by email, at that moment you give us the key that lets us recompute all the dedup_hashes that correspond to you (one for each potential day, from 0 to 730) and delete the related records. Before your email, on the server we see only apparently random numbers and do not know who they belong to.

For this reason, we cannot identify you if you write without your install_id. If you uninstalled the app before saving it elsewhere (or if you reset it via "Erase all data"), the install_id is lost forever and your data on the server becomes effectively anonymous even to us: write to us anyway and we will try to delete based on other criteria (e.g., approximate date of first and last reception), but we cannot guarantee complete deletion of your specific records if we cannot distinguish them with certainty from those of other users.

4. What remains after deletion

Aggregated statistical distributions (e.g., "the average Dopa Index of users after 30 days of use is X") may remain in our systems even after deletion of your individual payloads, because they are anonymous aggregated data (without the individual dedup_hash key) containing no records referable to you.

5. Legal references

Right to erasure: Art. 17 of EU Regulation 2016/679 (GDPR). For all other rights (access, rectification, restriction, objection, portability, complaint to the Italian Data Protection Authority), see Privacy Policy §7.
